What Is Bite Correction?

Bite correction, also known as malocclusion treatment, addresses the improper alignment between your upper and lower teeth when your jaw is closed. A misaligned bite can cause difficulties in chewing, speaking, and can lead to jaw pain, headaches, and uneven tooth wear.

With over 40 years of orthodontic expertise, Dr. Photjana and our team have successfully treated thousands of complex bite cases, from mild misalignments to cases requiring surgical intervention.

Types of Bite Issues We Treat

Overbite

Upper front teeth overlap lower teeth excessively

Underbite

Lower teeth extend past upper front teeth

Crossbite

Upper teeth sit inside lower teeth when biting

Open Bite

Front teeth don't meet when back teeth are closed

Deep Bite

Upper teeth cover too much of lower teeth

Jaw Misalignment

Upper and lower jaws don't align properly

Treatment Options

  • Traditional Braces: Most effective for moderate to severe bite issues
  • Clear Aligners: For mild to moderate cases where discretion is preferred
  • Palate Expanders: For crossbites caused by narrow upper jaw
  • Orthognathic Surgery: For severe cases requiring jaw repositioning

Why Early Treatment Matters

While bite correction can be performed at any age, early intervention (typically between ages 7-14) can take advantage of the jaw's natural growth, often resulting in shorter treatment times and better outcomes. For adults, modern techniques and technology have made bite correction more comfortable and efficient than ever before.
